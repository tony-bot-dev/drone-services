### 3. FAA Requirements

**Part 107 Certification:**
- Required for any commercial drone operation
- Test fee: $175
- Must pass FAA Part 107 Aeronautical Knowledge Test
- Remote pilot certificate required

**Waivers Needed for Events:**
- **Night operations** — Requires waiver from FAA (can take months)
- **Flying over people** — Category 1-4 waivers or operations under Part 107 Subpart D
- **TFR (Temporary Flight Restrictions)** — Common near stadiums during events
- **Scottsdale Airport (SDL) / Phoenix Sky Harbor (PHX)** — Class B airspace, requires authorization via LAANC

**Arizona Specific:**
- State law allows local municipalities to regulate drone use
- Phoenix city permits may be required for commercial operations

### 7. Insurance & Liability

**Requirements:**
- **Minimum $1 million liability** for commercial operations (standard)
- **$2 million** often required by venues/events
- ** Hull coverage** for owned drones ($5,000-20,000 per drone)

**Costs:**
- Annual liability ($1M): ~$2,000-5,000/year
- Per-event coverage: $500-2,000/day
- On-demand hourly: ~$25/hour (SkyWatch, AirModo)

## Phase 3: Key Learnings

1. **Market gap:** No dedicated event security drone services in Arizona — light shows dominate the drone industry
2. **FAA barrier:** Part 107 is baseline; night waivers and airspace authorizations are the real hurdles
3. **Pricing:** $1,500-10,000/day range is realistic for event work
4. **Insurance:** $1M liability is minimum; venues often require $2M
5. **Differentiation:** Position as "security/surveillance" not "drone photography" — different market, less competition
